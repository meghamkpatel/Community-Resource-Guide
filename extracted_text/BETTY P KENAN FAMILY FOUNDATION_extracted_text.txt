Owen Graham Kenan
Staff Writer
Wilmington StarNews
Kenan was Vice Chairman of the Board of Directors of Flagler System Inc., Palm Beach, Florida, a family-owned business that operates The Breakers Hotel and related real estate interests in Palm Beach.
Kenan was president of Kenan Enterprises based in Chapel Hill and was a former Vice Chairman of the Board of Directors of Kenan Transport Company.
He was a Trustee of The University of North Carolina at Wilmington, The Henry Morrison Flagler Museum, Palm Beach, Florida and The Liberty Hall Restoration Commission, Kenansville, North Carolina.
Kenan was also a Trustee and/or Director of The Kenan Family Foundation, the four William R. Kenan Jr. Funds that support The Frank H. Kenan Institute of Private Enterprise, The Kenan Institute for the Arts, The Kenan Institute for Engineering, Technology and Science and The Kenan Institute for Ethics, and he was a director of the Good Samaritan Hospital, West Palm Beach, Florida, the North Carolina Zoological Society and a trustee of The Randleigh Foundation Trust that gives strong support to the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill Libraries,
He was a very active member of the Kenan Institute Asia located in Bangkok, Thailand where he traveled extensively and was also an active member of the Board of Visitors of the Kenan-Flagler Business School. He was recently awarded the Global Leadership Award by the Kenan-Flagler Business School.
Kenan was born in Charleston, South Carolina the son of the late Frank Hawkins Kenan and Harriet DuBose Gray. He is survived by his wife Sterling Holt Kenan, a son Frank Hawkins Kenan II, two daughters Roberta Sterling Kenan and Holt DuBose Kenan, a brother Thomas S. Kenan III, two half sisters Liza Kenan Howell and Annice Hawkins Kenan, his stepmother Betty P. Kenan and his stepbrother Owen Gwyn Jr.
Funeral services will be held at 2:00 p.m. on Thursday at The Chapel of the Cross in Chapel Hill. A graveside service is scheduled for Friday, September 6 at 11:00 a.m. at the Graham Kenan plot in Oakdale Cemetery, Wilmington, North Carolina. In lieu of flowers, the family requests contributions be made to the Owen Graham Kenan Scholarship Fund at The University of North Carolina at Wilmington Foundation, 601 South College Road, Wilmington, North Carolina, 28403-5905.
Howerton & Bryan Funeral Home, Durham, is in charge of funeral arrangements.
